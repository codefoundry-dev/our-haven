# Miami/Florida as launch jurisdiction; multi-state pluggability where it pays for itself

**Status:** **superseded by ADR-0009 (2026-05-26) — v1 now launches as a US-national marketplace from day one with per-state compliance adapters as core deliverables; the Florida soft-launch concentration is dropped.** Previously: accepted (2026-05-11, supersedes the UK framing accepted 2026-05-08). Background-check sub-decision partially superseded 2026-05-19 by ADR-0007.

> Historical note: this ADR previously named the United Kingdom as the launch jurisdiction (DBS / HCPC / Ofsted VCR / UK GDPR / GBP / PSD2-FCA SCA / UK VAT). On 2026-05-11 the launch jurisdiction pivoted to **Miami, Florida**, with Florida statewide compliance built in and design hooks for near-term US expansion. UK references in this ADR have been rewritten in place rather than superseded with a new ADR.

## Context

The original project plan said the app would be "available everywhere from day one, but marketing and outreach concentrate on a single market." That phrasing is jurisdiction-agnostic, but vendor selection, regulator surfaces, the data-protection regime, and the financial-compliance surface all snap to the chosen jurisdiction the moment v1 ships. Discovery initially landed on the United Kingdom; on 2026-05-11 the client pivoted to launching in **Miami, Florida** instead, with Florida statewide regulatory scope and US expansion as a near-term Phase 2 ambition.

The launch jurisdiction is not a cosmetic detail — it dictates the background-check vendor, the Provider classification regime, the data-protection regime, the regulator landscape for Specialists, and the whole shape of the financial-compliance work.

## Decision

**v1 launches with Miami, Florida as the soft-launch market and Florida statewide compliance configured.** Currency is **USD**. The technical and compliance design is shaped for Florida regulation, with **state-pluggable compliance modules** where the marginal cost of pluggability is low, so that adding a second state in Phase 2 is a configuration exercise rather than a re-platforming exercise.

- **Background check (rewritten 2026-05-19 — see ADR-0007):** **Checkr's standard package** (county criminal 7-year + national criminal database + national sex offender registry + SSN trace). ~$30 per check, charged at **$35** to the Provider. This is **marketplace-grade**, not statutory Florida Level 2. Statutory FL Level 2 (FBI + FDLE fingerprint, AHCA/DCF clearinghouse, Fla. Stat. §§ 402.305 / 402.3055 / 435.04) was the original v1 choice but does not actually apply to independent in-home Providers and is incompatible with the client's stated $35 cost target. The background-check integration module is built behind a vendor-agnostic interface so a different vendor — or a future voluntary upgrade to statutory Level 2 — is a configuration change rather than a rebuild.
- **Specialist license verification:** manual admin verification against Florida public registers via the **FL Department of Health (DOH) MQA license verification portal** and the **FL Department of Business and Professional Regulation (DBPR)** where applicable:
  - **Speech-Language Pathologists** → FL Board of Speech-Language Pathology and Audiology (DOH).
  - **Occupational Therapists** → FL Board of Occupational Therapy Practice (DOH).
  - **Psychologists** → FL Board of Psychology (DOH).
  - **ABA practitioners (BCBAs)** → FL Board of Behavior Analysis (DOH); ABA has been a statutorily licensed profession in Florida since 2022, so unlike the UK no enhanced manual scrutiny is required — license number lookup is authoritative.
  - **Physicians (MD/DO)** → FL Board of Medicine / FL Board of Osteopathic Medicine (DOH).
  - **Nurses (RN/LPN)** → FL Board of Nursing (DOH).
  - License-board lookup is wrapped in a per-state adapter (Florida first; second-state adapter is a Phase 2 addition).
- **Childcare-program registrations** — Florida has no direct analogue to Ofsted's Voluntary Childcare Register that signals tax-benefit eligibility to parents. The UK "Ofsted-registered badge" feature is **replaced** with a **CDCTC-eligibility toggle** on Nanny and Babysitter profiles: a Provider who self-attests they will issue **IRS Form W-10** (Dependent Care Provider's Identification and Certification) gets a "Tax-credit-friendly" badge, enabling Parents who plan to claim the **Child and Dependent Care Tax Credit** or use a **Dependent Care FSA** to filter for it. Self-attestation only in v1; verification deferred. Florida-specific licensure for **Family Child Care Homes (FCCH)** under DCF child-care licensing is surfaced as an *optional* uploaded credential for the small minority of Providers who operate FCCHs (most v1 Providers are in-home Nannies/Babysitters and are exempt from FL DCF licensing).
- **Data protection:** No single US omnibus law — Our Haven operates under a sectoral patchwork: **Florida Digital Bill of Rights (FDBR)** for consumer rights and data protection assessments where thresholds apply; **COPPA** for any personal information processing that touches a child under 13 (note: Children do not authenticate, but data *about* Children is collected from Parents — best practice is to treat the platform as COPPA-aware even when the strict operator-collects-from-child trigger doesn't fire); **HIPAA** for Specialist clinical sessions and special-needs notes that may constitute PHI (Specialists are treated as the covered entity for clinical notes; the platform avoids being a Business Associate by not requiring Specialists to upload clinical notes — they remain in the Specialist's own EHR); **FIPA** (Florida Information Protection Act 2014) for breach notification. **External US privacy counsel** engaged before launch (replaces the UK fractional DPO engagement). A **Privacy Impact Assessment (PIA)** — equivalent in posture to the UK DPIA, and required as a "data protection assessment" under FDBR if thresholds are met — is authored before Phase 4 launch. Article 9-style **explicit consent** for special-needs flags on Child profiles is retained as a UX pattern even though no single US statute requires the exact form; it is the cleanest way to satisfy COPPA-aware best practice, HIPAA-adjacent prudence, and emerging state laws simultaneously.
- **Data residency:** **GCP US region** (Cloud Run, Cloud SQL, Cloud Storage, Cloud Tasks in `us-east1` or `us-east4`; Firestore in `nam5` US multi-region; Firebase Auth on the US identity pool). US privacy law does not generally mandate residency, but co-locating all personal data in US regions keeps the FDBR data-flow inventory clean and avoids cross-border-transfer questions for early users.
- **Payments:** Stripe Connect Express **US entity**. **3DS is supported but not mandatory** (no PSD2/SCA equivalent in the US); 3DS is used opportunistically for fraud reduction on high-risk transactions, not on every Parent payment. Stripe Tax handles **Florida sales tax** computation (childcare services are generally exempt under FL sales tax statutes; the Parent Subscription is a digital/access fee that FL does not currently tax; Commission is B2B-service and not sales-taxable in FL). **Form 1099-K** issuance to Providers is handled by Stripe Connect; no separate UK VAT registration equivalent. **Sales tax registration with the Florida Department of Revenue** is a Phase 0 call — likely not required given the exempt status of in-scope services, but counsel-confirmed before launch.
- **Provider classification:** US worker classification under the **IRS 20-factor / common-law test** (federal) plus Florida's state-specific factors for unemployment compensation. Florida is comparatively contractor-friendly; Providers are structured as 1099 independent contractors. Multi-state expansion will require state-by-state classification review (notably **California's AB5 / ABC test** if expansion includes CA) — wrap the classification language in the Provider Terms behind a per-state addendum pattern.
- **Geographic scope at launch:** the app is available throughout **Florida** from day one; soft-launch marketing concentrates on **Miami-Dade** (Miami metro). Adjacent counties (Broward, Palm Beach) are available but unmarketed. Provider sign-up is geofenced to Florida postcodes / addresses; an out-of-state sign-up is rejected with a "we're not yet operating in [state]" message.

If the launch base changes (e.g., to a different state, or to multi-state simultaneously), this ADR is superseded and **the per-state adapter set is extended; currency and federal compliance design stay; state-specific compliance, regulator surfaces, and sales-tax configuration are added per the new jurisdiction.**

## Why

- **Florida has a well-defined childcare background-check regime** (statutory Level 2 via FDLE / AHCA Clearinghouse) and a major commercial vendor (Checkr) with native marketplace integration. v1 uses Checkr's *standard* package rather than the statutory regime — see ADR-0007 for that sub-decision — but the vendor choice and the multi-state pluggability rationale are unchanged.
- **Florida has clean public license registers** (DOH MQA portal, DBPR portal) for manual Specialist verification — eliminates the need for a paid verification-vendor integration in v1, exactly as the UK alternative would have.
- **US payment compliance is structurally simpler than the UK/EU.** No PSD2-mandated SCA on every transaction. 3DS becomes a fraud tool, not a compliance tool. Stripe Connect Express's US entity handles 1099-K reporting automatically. Stripe Tax handles state-by-state sales tax where it applies.
- **Florida is comparatively contractor-friendly.** The 1099 independent-contractor structure for Providers carries less misclassification risk in FL than it would in (say) California. This makes Florida a sensible first-state for a marketplace whose supply economics depend on contractor status.
- **Single-state, single-language, single-currency simplification.** USD only, English only (Spanish is a likely Phase 2 add given Miami's Hispanic population, but is not v1 scope). No FX work, no multi-currency Stripe configuration.
- **Miami is the soft-launch market, not the geographic scope.** Florida-wide compliance is built; Miami concentration is a marketing decision, not a build decision. This avoids the trap of city-scoped engineering (postcode whitelists, etc.) that would have to be torn out the moment Ci'erro markets in Tampa or Orlando.
- **State-pluggable compliance modules where cheap.** The background-check integration and the license-board lookup are wrapped behind vendor-agnostic interfaces because the marginal cost of doing so at v1 is small and the Phase 2 saving is large. The Verification workflow itself (a deep module) is state-agnostic — it consumes verification results, not vendor APIs.

## Considered alternatives

- **Miami only, Florida-narrow engineering.** Faster to build but creates a city-scoped data model (Miami postcodes whitelisted, etc.) that would have to be torn out within months. Rejected.
- **US-wide launch on day one.** State background-check regimes vary widely (FL Level 2 ≠ NY fingerprint check ≠ CA Live Scan); Specialist licensure registers are per-state; worker classification is per-state. Multi-state on day one multiplies vendor adapters, sales-tax configurations, and counsel engagements — incompatible with the 16-week timeline.
- **Stay with the UK plan.** Workable engineering-wise but moot given the client's market decision.
- **Single non-Florida US state** (e.g., Texas, New York). All would have shifted vendor adapter selection and license-register integrations. Florida is the client's working market; not a technical preference.

## Consequences

- **Every Florida-specific design choice in `CONTEXT.md`** (DOH/DBPR Specialist boards, FDBR + COPPA + HIPAA framing, USD, FL sales tax model) is conditional on this ADR. The background-screening posture is **no longer Florida-specific** (it's vendor-grade Checkr, applicable in any US state — see ADR-0007). A change in launch state is **not** a small change, but is **smaller** than a change of country thanks to the state-pluggable module design.
- Phase 0 carries a critical confirmation: Ci'erro must formally confirm Miami, Florida as the soft-launch market and Florida as the compliance jurisdiction. If she names a different state, the per-state adapter set re-points; the federal compliance design (HIPAA, COPPA, 1099-K, FDBR-style PIA) is reused with minor adjustments.
- Future readers looking at the codebase and seeing Florida-specific vendor integrations should land on this ADR first to understand why the project does not appear locale-agnostic, and to find the per-state adapter contract.
- Provider sign-up is geofenced to **Florida** at launch (not just Miami) — sign-up from an out-of-state address is rejected with a clear "we're not yet operating in [state]" message.
- The historical UK posture (DBS, HCPC, Ofsted VCR, UK GDPR, GBP, PSD2-FCA SCA, UK VAT, fractional DPO) is preserved as a working assumption in version-control history only; it is not active anywhere in the live design.
