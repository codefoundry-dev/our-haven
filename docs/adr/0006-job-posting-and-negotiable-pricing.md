# Job-posting marketplace + negotiable pricing via structured Offers

**Status:** accepted (2026-05-18). **Partially superseded 2026-05-19** — see Historical note.

> **Historical note (2026-05-19).** The client sync on 2026-05-19 killed the slot-pick entry path: Provider profiles no longer surface per-slot calendars (see `CONTEXT.md` § Availability — Availability is now a general weekly summary grid + free-text note). The replacement entry path is **Direct-Message + Book-request acceptance**; the canonical Job → Application → Offer → Booking chain (Decision §1) is preserved by **lazy materialisation at acceptance time** rather than slot-pick-time creation. Decisions §2, §3, §4, §5, §6, §9 below have been rewritten in place to reflect this. The broader rationale (Job as canonical anchor, structured Offer primitive, negotiable pricing, caps and gates on Posted Jobs, free-text Job descriptions with consent acknowledgement) is unchanged.

## Context

The v1 design as specified in PRD-0001 and `CONTEXT.md` (pre-2026-05-18) had two shape-defining properties:

1. **Single discovery path: slot-pick from Provider Availability.** A Parent browsed a Provider's profile, picked a slot from the Provider's published calendar, the slot blocked-on-request, and the Provider had 24h to accept. There was no "reverse" path — Parents could not describe a need and have Providers come to them.
2. **Unilateral pricing.** The Provider's Published Rate was the price. The Pricing & Commission calculator consumed a single Rate input. Negotiation did not exist as a concept.

Both properties had reasoned defenders. Slot-pick is the high-volume Babysitter path (the closest analog to Care.com competitor behaviour) and gets a Parent from "I need a sitter Saturday" to "booked" in two minutes. Unilateral pricing simplifies the Pricing calculator, removes a dispute-attack surface, and matches how the simplest end of the babysitter market actually works.

Two pressures from the client conversation on 2026-05-18 reshape both:

- **Specialist and recurring-Nanny demand maps poorly to slot-pick.** A Parent looking for an ABA therapist with weekday-morning availability for their 6-year-old does not want to browse twelve Specialist calendars and pick a slot speculatively; they want to describe the need once and let qualified Specialists self-select. The current model forces a clinical-fit conversation into a calendar grid.
- **Real childcare pricing is negotiated, not posted.** Parents and Providers commonly agree on Rate-per-engagement based on context (multi-child surcharge waivers, longer-engagement discounts, special-needs case complexity, recurring-arrangement loyalty pricing). A "Provider publishes Rate, Parent pays Rate" model is a constraint the market itself does not actually impose; encoding it makes the platform feel inflexible compared to the back-channel deals Parents and Providers do off-platform.

This ADR introduces a unified architectural model that satisfies the new pressures without abandoning the slot-pick UX or the simplicity of the existing Booking state machine.

## Decision

1. **Job becomes the canonical anchor object.** Every Parent-Provider transaction in v1 traces back to a Job, an Application, and an accepted Offer. This is a schema-level commitment: there is no path from Parent to Booking that bypasses the Job → Application → Offer chain.

2. **Two ways a Job comes into being, one downstream chain (rewritten 2026-05-19):**
   - **Posted Job.** Parent composes (Category, ZIP, scope, free-text description, optional budget hint), publishes, Providers in matching Category and area apply.
   - **Direct-Message Job.** Parent opens a chat with a specific Provider (from search results or the Provider profile) and the two exchange messages and structured Book-requests (Offers — see Decision §5; the parent-facing UI label for an Offer is **"Book"** / **"Send Booking Request"**). The Job does **not** exist while the chat is in pre-acceptance state — the thread is anchored to a `thread_id`, not a `job_id`. At the moment one party hits **Accept** on a Book-request, the system **atomically materialises** Job + Application + Booking; the thread rebinds from `thread_id` to `job_id`. The Parent and Provider never see the Job UI for Direct-Message; it is plumbing that unifies the schema. Replaces the prior **slot-pick Job** entry path, which was removed 2026-05-19 because Provider profiles no longer surface per-slot calendars.

3. **Job lifecycle (refined 2026-05-19):** `draft → open → (awarded | expired | cancelled) → closed`. **Posted Jobs** transit `draft → open` and auto-expire 14 days after publishing. **Direct-Message Jobs skip `draft` and `open` entirely** — they are born in `awarded` state at the moment of Book-request acceptance, with one Application already filed (carrying the accepted Offer). The 14-day expiry and the 15-Application cap (Decision §7) apply to Posted Jobs only.

4. **Application** = Provider's response to an open Job (Posted-Job flow), or the auto-Application materialised when a Direct-Message Book-request is accepted (Direct-Message flow). Refined 2026-05-19. **Posted-Job applications** carry the Provider's first Offer plus a free-text proposal; one Application per Provider per Job; filing creates a Message thread anchored to the Job ID. **Direct-Message applications** are created lazily at acceptance time, carry the accepted Offer as their single Offer, and have no separate free-text proposal — the chat history is the proposal (see `CONTEXT.md` § Message).

5. **Offer** is the structured price-and-scope primitive. Either party can send. Fields: `proposed_rate`, `scope_type` (hourly | per_session), `scope_quantity`, `scope_note` (≤280 chars free text), `computed_total` (`proposed_rate × scope_quantity` + per-child surcharge snapshot from sender-side Provider profile at send time), `valid_until` (default 72h), `sender` (parent | provider). Renders as an **Offer bubble** in the Message thread with **Accept / Counter / Decline** pill buttons. Accept creates a Booking with `agreed_rate = proposed_rate`. Counter sends a new Offer back (previous marked `countered`). Decline closes. Untouched at `valid_until` → `expired`. **Anchor field (added 2026-05-19):** every Offer carries either a `job_id` (Posted-Job flow, or any Offer that exists post-Decision-§6 materialisation) or a `thread_id` (pre-acceptance Direct-Message flow). At Direct-Message acceptance, the accepted Offer's anchor flips from `thread_id` to the freshly-materialised `job_id`; predecessor counter-Offers in the same thread stay `thread_id`-anchored, viewable in chat history but not part of the materialised Job's audit trail. **UI labelling (added 2026-05-19):** parent-facing buttons render as **"Book"** / **"Send Booking Request"** / **"Accept Booking"** — "Offer" remains canonical in code, schema, and admin UI.

6. **Acceptance creates a Booking — in a flow-asymmetric initial state (refined 2026-05-19):**
   - **Posted-Job flow.** Parent Awards the Application → Booking is born in **`requested`** state with `agreed_rate` from the accepted Offer and the awarded Provider's `published_rate_at_creation_time` snapshotted for audit. The Provider has a 24h confirmation window before auto-decline (defense against speculative multi-Job applications by a Provider who can no longer deliver). The existing Booking state machine takes over from `requested` unchanged.
   - **Direct-Message flow.** The recipient's Accept click materialises Job + Application + Booking atomically; the Booking is born in **`accepted`** state directly. Both parties have committed by that click — the initiator committed when sending the Book-request, the recipient committed by accepting — so no `requested` interim exists. The existing Booking state machine takes over from `accepted` unchanged.

7. **Caps and gates:**
   - Per-Job cap: **15 Applications**, then the Job stops accepting new Applications.
   - Per-Provider cap: **30 Applications per calendar month**, reset on the 1st. Documented as a v1 starting number, re-tunable based on observed usage.
   - Posting a Job requires an active Parent **Subscription** (same gate as full search / messaging / booking).
   - Applying to a Job requires the Provider's **Verification** to be `cleared` (same gate as appearing in search).
   - Negotiation (sending Offers) requires the Parent to have an active Subscription and the Provider to be `cleared` — same gates as Job posting and Application filing respectively.

8. **Child profile disclosure on posted Jobs is free-text Parent-controlled.** Posted Jobs do not carry structured Child profile attachments. The Parent describes the need in free text; at compose time, the Parent acknowledges a one-time consent warning that the description is visible to every Provider who views the Job (timestamped on the Job record). Structured Child profile attachment happens at **Award** time, at which point the existing § Child profile visibility on Booking requests rules apply to the awarded Provider only.

9. **v1 deferrals (documented as Phase 2 candidates; updated 2026-05-19):**
   - **Slot-pick re-introduction (new 2026-05-19).** Per-slot calendars on Provider profiles were removed 2026-05-19 because Caregivers reported them impractical for managing dynamic schedules. If post-launch usage data shows clear demand for "tap-to-book a specific time without a chat", a Phase 2 re-introduction is possible — most likely scoped to Babysitters only (the original high-volume use case) and built on the same Job → Application → Offer chain via lazy materialisation, not the old slot-pick auto-Job plumbing.
   - **Direct invite to apply** (Parent reaches out to a specific Provider who didn't apply). v1 is closed-list — applications come from Providers who chose to apply.
   - **Per-Provider application credits / pay-per-lead** (Thumbtack model). v1 uses the monthly cap as a spam/quality control; pay-per-lead is a richer commercial mechanism evaluated post-launch based on Provider behaviour.
   - **Partial-award Jobs** (Parent awards multiple Providers from one Job, e.g. weekend-rotation Nanny coverage). v1 supports the same outcome via multiple Jobs.
   - **Single-account dual-role users** (a Provider booking a Tutor for their own child without a second account).
   - **Two-stage Job disclosure** (sanitised public brief + detailed brief unlocked for shortlisted Providers).

## Why

- **Single schema anchor (Decision 1) eliminates dual-path bookkeeping.** Reporting, Trust & Safety review, dispute audit, retention scheduling, Provider response-rate metrics all key off Job. The auto-Job for slot-picks costs one trivial record per slot-pick — negligible compared to the cost of maintaining two parallel transaction models.
- **Slot-pick UX is preserved verbatim (Decision 2 / Posted vs slot-pick split).** The Babysitter "Saturday night sitter in two taps" path is the highest-volume use case and the closest to Care.com competitor behaviour. Hiding the Job object from the Parent UI on slot-picks keeps that path identical to today's design.
- **Job-board flow unlocks the clinical and recurring-engagement use cases.** Specialists self-select against clinical fit by reading a Job description; recurring Nanny arrangements get described once instead of pieced together from a calendar.
- **Offers (Decision 5) survive disputes.** Free-form chat alone is he-said-she-said. Trust & Safety, dispute resolution, and the Pricing calculator all need a structured, timestamped, append-only record of what was agreed and when. The Offer bubble is the smallest thing that delivers this. Mirrors Upwork's "Send Offer" / Thumbtack's "Send a Quote" pattern, both of which converged on this primitive for the same reason.
- **Negotiation applies symmetrically to both paths (Decision 5)** — slot-pick auto-Offers can be countered by the Parent; posted Job Applications carry initial Offers that the Parent can counter. One Offer primitive everywhere keeps the Provider mobile companion inbox coherent (any incoming opportunity, regardless of origin, is an Offer to action).
- **Per-child surcharge snapshotting (Decision 5)** prevents in-flight Offers from drifting if the Provider edits their profile mid-negotiation. The Provider's profile is the source for the *default*; the Offer is the source for the *commitment*.
- **Free-text Job descriptions with consent warning (Decision 8)** extend the existing Parent-controlled consent model (`CONTEXT.md` § Sensitive-data consent) without inventing a new privacy primitive. Structured Child-profile attachment to a Job would create a *Provider-pre-Application access to structured special-needs flags* — a meaningfully different compliance surface than the existing post-acceptance disclosure. Free text keeps the disclosure call in the Parent's hands, where the consent model already lives. The two-stage public-brief / detailed-brief model is the right v2 architecture; v1 gets 80% of the privacy benefit at 20% of the build cost via the one-time consent acknowledgement.
- **15 / 30 caps (Decision 7)** are protective starting numbers. The per-Job cap protects Parent UX (a Job with 80 applications is unusable). The per-Provider cap is a v1 spam-control proxy for the pay-per-lead model deferred to v2 — it forces Provider self-selection (apply where you have a real shot) without introducing a new payment surface.
- **Subscription gate on Job posting (Decision 7)** keeps the marketplace's only Parent-side revenue mechanism intact. Letting unsubscribed Parents post Jobs to "test the waters" would create real engagement for Providers (who spend their cap allowance writing applications) against demand that never converts — a fairness problem on the supply side that would erode Provider trust in the new surface quickly.

## Considered alternatives

- **Keep slot-pick alongside the new Direct-Message path (considered 2026-05-19).** Rejected — Ci'erro reported caregivers find per-slot calendars impractical: real schedules are dynamic, published slots become stale, calendar maintenance is friction. Removing slot-pick simplifies the Provider profile (Availability is now a Mon–Sun × Morning/Afternoon/Evening grid + free-text note — see `CONTEXT.md` § Availability) and unifies discovery on the chat + Book-request flow. The capability is preserved as a Phase 2 re-introduction candidate (see Decision §9).
- **Slot-pick only (status quo).** Rejected — the Specialist and recurring-Nanny demand the client surfaced on 2026-05-18 is real and not served by a calendar-only model.
- **Job-posting only, deprecate slot-pick.** Rejected — kills the high-volume Babysitter "book a sitter in two taps" UX; forces every transaction through a write-a-description step that adds friction where none is needed; provides no upside for the use case where the Parent already knows which Provider they want.
- **Two parallel transaction models with no shared anchor object.** Considered. Rejected — every cross-cutting concern (T&S review, dispute audit, retention, reporting, the Provider opportunities inbox) would have to handle both paths separately. The Job anchor (Decision 1) costs one auto-record per slot-pick to eliminate all of that surface area.
- **Free-form chat negotiation, no structured Offer object.** Considered briefly. Rejected — disputes become impossible to adjudicate ("you said $35 in message 12"; "no, that was the *minimum* I'd consider"). The Offer object is the smallest thing that gives Trust & Safety a stable artifact to reason about.
- **Pay-per-lead Provider economics (Thumbtack model) in v1.** Considered. Rejected for v1, kept as v2 candidate. The build cost (Stripe purchase of application credits, wallet/balance UI, refund policy for unfair Parent behaviour, attribution-of-conversion logic for refunds) is real and the demand signal isn't strong enough pre-launch to justify it. The monthly cap is the v1 simplest-thing-that-works.
- **Job description includes a required structured `child_age_range` + `special_needs_flag` field.** Considered. Rejected because *Provider-pre-Application access to structured special-needs flags* is a meaningfully different compliance surface than the existing post-acceptance disclosure model and would require new consent text, new admin auditing, and a likely re-engagement with privacy counsel. Free-text-with-consent-warning keeps the disclosure within the Parent's existing consent posture.
- **Two-stage Job disclosure (public brief + Parent-unlocked detailed brief for shortlisted Providers).** This is the privacy-correct architecture for a v2 reverse-marketplace. Rejected for v1 on build cost (shortlist state, staged-application UI, two distinct application objects, "Parent has invited you to see the full brief" notification surface). Documented as a Phase 2 candidate.
- **Awarding a Job creates a Booking in `accepted` state directly.** Considered for UX simplicity. Rejected — the Provider has agreed to the price (via Offer) but the Booking's *slot/time* (especially for posted Jobs where no specific date was committed up front) still needs Provider confirmation. Routing the awarded Booking through `requested → accepted` keeps the existing state machine unmodified and gives the Provider one clean acceptance moment.

## Consequences

- **`CONTEXT.md` adds new terms: Job, Application, Offer.** Existing terms are revised: Rate (split into Published Rate + Agreed Rate), Booking (now traces to a Job, with Direct-Message variants born in `accepted` directly), Availability (**refined 2026-05-19** — general weekly summary grid + free-text note; no per-slot calendar), Subscription (Job posting added to gated features), Message (**refined 2026-05-19** — pre-Job threads allowed for pre-acceptance Direct-Message conversations), Child profile visibility (new sibling entry for Job posts), Notifications (new push and SMS events), **Video call (added 2026-05-19 — see ADR-0008)**.
- **The Pricing & Commission calculator's input changes**: it now consumes `agreed_rate` (from the accepted Offer) rather than the Provider's profile-level Published Rate. The calculator's signature and arithmetic are unchanged; only the source field flips. Existing tests are amended.
- **The Cancellation policy calculator is unaffected.** It already operated against the Booking's "estimated charge"; that estimate now derives from `agreed_rate` instead of Published Rate. No state-machine changes.
- **The Disintermediation detector now also runs on the `scope_note` field of every Offer.** Structured Offer fields (numbers) bypass the detector. Implementation: the Offer-submit path calls the same detection module the Message-submit path does, before persistence.
- **The Booking state machine is unchanged.** All new lifecycle complexity lives on the new Job and Application state machines; the convergence point (Awarded Application → Booking in `requested` state) is a transition the existing machine already handles.
- **Trust & Safety surface grows.** Offer bubbles join messages in the flagged-thread queue when their `scope_note` trips detection. The Trust & Safety audit log keys off Thread ID, which now keys off Job ID — every accessed thread implicitly identifies the Job it covers.
- **New domain modules to implement:**
  - **Job lifecycle state machine** (deep module — pure state graph).
  - **Application state machine** (deep module — pure state graph).
  - **Offer state machine** (deep module — pure; coordinates with Application and Booking on accept).
  - **Application-quota tracker** (per-Provider monthly cap accounting; deep module backed by a simple count + reset job).
- **PRD-0001 stories 23–34 (Parent Booking lifecycle), 40 (Provider sign-up), 48 (publish Availability), 50–54 (Booking inbox)** are partially superseded — they now describe one of two paths to a Booking. New stories must be added for Job composition, Application filing, Offer sending, counter-Offer flow, Job awarding, Parent's "my Jobs" surface, Provider's Opportunities feed. A PRD v1.1 revision precedes scope-lock.
- **DESIGN.md must add:**
  - New components: **Offer bubble** message variant; **Job card** (for both Parent's "my Jobs" list and Provider's Opportunities feed); **Application card** (for Parent's "Applications on this Job" list); **Status pill** variants for Job lifecycle and Application lifecycle.
  - New pages: Parent Job-compose (multi-step with consent warning); Parent "My Jobs" list; Parent Job detail with Applications list; Parent Award flow; Provider Opportunities feed (Open Jobs + My Applications tabs); Provider Application composer; Provider Application detail.
  - The Provider mobile companion surface from ADR-0005 inherits all the Job-side affordances.
- **Phase timeline impact.** The Job-board build (server: state machines + cap accounting + quota tracker; client: Parent + Provider surfaces; new components in DESIGN.md) is a Phase 3-sized chunk of work in its own right. Combined with the Provider mobile companion (ADR-0005), the original Phase 3 timeline must be re-baselined. Recommended decomposition: the slot-pick auto-Job plumbing ships first (preserves existing UX, lays the schema), then Job-board posting flow, then negotiation Offers, then Provider Opportunities feed. The work parallelises poorly because every layer keys off the schema below it.
- **Privacy & legal review impact.** Free-text Job descriptions are a new disclosure surface for child information. Privacy counsel (in-progress per `CONTEXT.md` § Privacy counsel) should review the Job-compose consent text and the auditability of the timestamped acknowledgement before launch. The Privacy Policy needs a new paragraph describing how Job descriptions are visible to Providers and how the Parent retains control over what they include.
