## Problem Statement

Parents across the United States looking for childcare and child-development support — Babysitters, Tutors, Nannies (**Caregivers**) and clinical **Providers** (speech, ABA, occupational therapy, psychology, …) — face a fragmented, unverified market. Existing options (Care.com, agency listings, word-of-mouth Facebook groups, neighborhood referrals) range from un-vetted to expensive, and none of them are built around the specific needs of families with neurodivergent children, where matching the right person to a child's needs is a judgement call, not a search-and-click.

The supply side also faces friction, in two different shapes (ADR-0011). **Caregivers** lack a national marketplace that combines **marketplace-grade Checkr background screening** (county criminal + national sex offender + SSN), trusted discovery, and **Stripe-handled same-day Payouts** in one place; they lose time and money setting up their own profiles, vetting prospects, and chasing payment. **Clinical Providers** lack a credible, low-overhead way to list their practice and fill a schedule without standing up their own booking site — and need their license cross-checked against the **right state professional board** for their resident state (with per-state license-board adapters built in) so families can trust the credential. Keeping clinical discussion and payment **off-platform** (a deliberate HIPAA posture — see ADR-0011) is a feature for them, not a gap.

The result: Parents settle for whoever is convenient rather than appropriate; Caregivers and Providers churn through admin overhead instead of doing their work.

## Solution

Our Haven is a **US-national** two-sided marketplace from day one (per ADR-0009 — supersedes the ADR-0003 Miami/Florida soft-launch posture), built on a state-pluggable compliance core so the platform routes verification, sales-tax, classification-addendum, and privacy-rights surfaces per the user's resident state without engineering rework as supply emerges from new states.

**A single React Native + Expo mobile app serves Parents and both supply roles** in role-aware shells; the role is chosen at sign-up via a **3-tab role-pick screen** — *"I'm a Parent / I'm a Caregiver / I'm a Provider"* (re-grilled 2026-05-30 per ADR-0011 — the third tab's label moves from "Specialist" to "Provider"). The three tabs map to **three flat top-level roles** — `role ∈ {parent, caregiver, provider}`; the former `kind` discriminator is gone. A **Caregiver** carries `category ∈ {babysitter, tutor, nanny}`; a **Provider** (the clinical tier) carries `specialty ∈ {slp, ot, aba, psychology, …}`. The two supply roles are **different businesses** and fork on discovery, booking, money, and compliance (ADR-0011):

- **Caregivers** are the transactional **payment rail.** Parents discover them via **unified search → message-first**, or by **posting a Job** that Caregivers apply to; engagement is **hourly and negotiable** (Job → Application → Offer → Booking); the platform takes a **Commission** and pays the Caregiver via **Stripe Connect** (same-day target). Caregivers run the day-to-day workflow on mobile (accept Bookings, manage Availability, apply to Jobs, message, complete Sessions).
- **Providers** (clinical) are a **listings + scheduling SaaS.** Parents **browse the profile and book an open consultation slot** directly (per-session, fixed display Rate, no Job/Offer/negotiation); **clinical discussion and payment happen off-platform** (HIPAA) — **no Stripe Connect, no Commission, no Payout.** The Provider is monetized by a **Provider Subscription** (individual / small business, self-serve) or a sales-led **custom contract** (large corporation, via a Contact-Us intake).

The **web portal remains the system of record** for heavy onboarding (Caregiver Stripe Connect KYC, supply-side license/insurance uploads) and Caregiver Payout management; the mobile companion links out to web in an in-app browser for those tasks.

**Every Caregiver transaction traces back to a canonical chain: Job → Application → Offer → Booking** (per ADR-0006, narrowed to Caregivers by ADR-0011). Two paths produce Jobs (refined 2026-05-19): Parents may **post a Job** describing what they need (Caregivers in the matching Category and area apply with a structured Offer); OR Parents may **open a Direct-Message chat** with a specific Caregiver, negotiate via free-text + structured **Book-requests** (the parent-facing UI label for an Offer), and on acceptance the system **lazily materialises** Job + Application + Booking atomically. **Pricing is negotiable** — either side can send a Book-request/Offer; Offers carry Accept/Counter/Decline pill buttons; acceptance creates a Booking with the Agreed Rate baked in. Posted-Job Bookings start in `requested` (Caregiver has 24h to confirm); Direct-Message Bookings start in `accepted` directly (the accepting click is the commitment). *(Naming note: the client calls the Posted-Job path "Direct Booking" and the search→message path "Search & Message"; our canonical term for the search→message path is **Direct-Message** — opposite senses of "direct". Code keeps "Direct-Message"; client-facing copy needs one consistent vocabulary — flagged in Further Notes.)*

**Provider (clinical) bookings do not use the Job chain at all.** A Provider publishes **consultation slots**; a Parent books an open slot directly from the profile — **slot-pick, resurrected for the Provider role only** (ADR-0011). The booking is a per-session schedule entry with **no Job, no Application, no Offer, and no payment intent** (off-platform). Caregiver profiles, by contrast, display only a general weekly availability summary (Day × Morning/Afternoon/Evening grid + free-text note) and carry no bookable slots.

An **admin dashboard** runs Provider review, Trust & Safety message access, dispute resolution, and platform metrics.

The marketplace runs on **two subscription products** plus a Caregiver Commission (ADR-0011): a **Parent Subscription** (web-billed; unlocks full search beyond the preview, messaging, sending Book-requests, posting Jobs, and booking Provider consultations) and a **Provider Subscription** (the clinical tier's listing fee — individual / small-business self-serve, or a corporate **Contact-Us** custom contract). Caregiver revenue is a **percentage Commission** skimmed from each Caregiver Booking via Stripe Connect's application fee; **Provider consultations carry no Commission** (payment is off-platform). Supply-side classification (1099 independent contractor under IRS common-law test as the federal baseline, with **per-state classification addenda** for AB5/ABC-test states), the US privacy patchwork (federal floor: COPPA + HIPAA-adjacent + FCRA; **state-privacy patchwork module** routing CCPA/CPRA + VCDPA + CPA + CTDPA + UCPA + FDBR + OCPA + TDPSA + others per user state), explicit Parent consent for the family profile's **Safety Behaviors** checklist (sensitive child data — re-anchored from the removed Child profile per ADR-0012), and **Stripe Tax** per-state nexus + 1099-K reporting are first-class concerns built into v1, not afterthoughts.

A public Parent web app ships in Phase 6 from the same React Native codebase (via React Native Web).

3. As a Parent, I want to see and explicitly accept a sensitive-information consent screen before I can save any **Safety Behaviors** on my family **Parent profile**, so that my child's sensitive data is handled with COPPA-aware, HIPAA-adjacent, and state-privacy-compliant care. *(v1.4 (2026-05-30, ADR-0012): the consent moment re-anchors from the removed Child profile onto editing the family-level Safety Behaviors checklist — now a bounded fixed checklist, not free-text clinical notes; consent is unchanged in force.)*

As a Parent, I want a single **family-level Parent profile** — a **Bio** (free-text family info), **Preferences** (a checklist of desired Caregiver traits, e.g. non-smoker / pet-friendly), and a fixed **Safety Behaviors** checklist (aggression, self-injurious behaviour, wandering / running off, … — final list from Ci'erro) — so that Caregivers I engage understand my family without the platform collecting a detailed per-child record. *(v1.4 (2026-05-30, ADR-0012): replaces the former multi-Child-profile model. See `CONTEXT.md` § Subscription wall.)*
7. As a Parent, I want to subscribe to Our Haven via a web checkout (linked from the mobile app), so that I can unlock full search (lifting the preview blur), messaging, sending Book-requests, Job posting, **and booking Provider consultations**. *(v1.1: Subscription gate extended to Job posting per ADR-0006. v1.4 (2026-05-30, ADR-0011): also gates booking a Provider consultation — the only platform revenue on the Parent↔Provider clinical interaction, since the consultation itself is off-platform.)*

9. As a Parent, I want to apply a discount code to my Subscription, so that I can take advantage of launch promotions.

## Implementation Decisions

### Backend stack and cross-platform communication

Per ADR-0004 (Node + TS + Fastify + OpenAPI-first + Postgres-as-system-of-record, §§1–3 + §8 carried forward) and **ADR-0010** (platform stack — supersedes ADR-0004 §§4–7):

- **Backend runtime: Node.js + TypeScript.** Best SDK fit for Stripe Connect, Stripe Tax, Twilio, Resend, Supabase, Daily.co, and Checkr (the v1 background-check vendor). Backend ↔ web frontend share types via OpenAPI codegen.
- **API protocol: OpenAPI-first REST + JSON.** The OpenAPI spec is the source of truth; typed TypeScript clients are generated for the React Native mobile app, the Provider web portal, and the admin dashboard. CI fails on spec drift.
- **Data plane (single store, ADR-0010):**
  - **PostgreSQL on Supabase** is the system of record for everything — Bookings, Sessions, Payments, Ratings, Verifications, message content (for retention/audit), audit logs, retention bookkeeping.
  - **Supabase Realtime** delivers live messaging by subscribing to row inserts on the `messages` table. New Messages are written to Postgres canonically (disintermediation-redacted) and the Realtime stream broadcasts the redacted row to subscribed clients — no separate write-fanout layer.
- **Hosting (ADR-0010):** Backend (Fastify) runs on **Fly.io `iad`** (Ashburn, VA). **Supabase US-region project** hosts Auth + Postgres + Realtime + Storage. **Vercel US-region** hosts the Provider web portal and admin dashboard (both Next.js). US data residency under ADR-0009 is enforced by region configuration on every service.
- **Background jobs: `pgmq`** for delayed jobs (Booking 24h expiry, Session 24h auto-confirm, Dispute window expiry, retention/erasure scheduled runs) and **`pg_cron`** for periodic tasks — both first-class Supabase Postgres extensions. Time-delayed work MUST go through `pgmq` — in-process timers are forbidden because Fly.io may restart instances during deploys or scale events.
- **File storage: Supabase Storage** for ID uploads, license documents, state home-childcare registration certificates, and profile photos. Clients upload via signed URLs issued by the backend; downloads are also via short-lived signed URLs scoped to the requesting actor's permissions.

### Cross-platform communication shape

- **Parent + Provider React Native app** → backend over **HTTPS REST** using the generated TypeScript client. Auth via **Supabase access token** in the `Authorization` header; the backend's auth plugin verifies the JWT locally with the project JWT secret on every request. **Live messaging** events delivered via Supabase Realtime row-level subscriptions (`@supabase/supabase-js`), not polled over REST.
- **Provider web portal (Next.js on Vercel)** → backend over **HTTPS REST** using the generated TypeScript client. Auth via Supabase access token (email/password or Google). Live messaging via Supabase Realtime web SDK. **Step-up MFA** required at the backend for payout-sensitive endpoints (changing bank details, initiating withdrawals).
- **Admin dashboard (Next.js on Vercel)** → backend over **HTTPS REST** using the same generated TypeScript client (different auth scope). **Mandatory TOTP MFA** enforced server-side on every request (not just at sign-in) for sensitive operations.
- **Stripe webhooks** → dedicated backend endpoint with Stripe signature verification → translated into Booking lifecycle / Subscription / Payout events for the deep modules.
- **Background-check webhooks (Checkr in v1)** → dedicated backend endpoint with vendor signature verification → translated into Verification workflow events. Endpoint is implemented behind a vendor-agnostic interface so a second-state vendor can be added in Phase 2.
- **Daily.co webhooks** (optional, for call telemetry) → dedicated backend endpoint.
- **Outbound notifications** flow out through the Notifications dispatcher: Expo Push (mobile push via FCM/APNs), VAPID web push (Provider portal + admin), Resend (email), Twilio (SMS).

### Modules

The implementation is organized as **deep modules** (pure logic, simple interfaces, isolated tests) plus integration modules wrapping external SDKs. The original v1 set of nine modules is preserved; **four modules** (Job / Application / Offer / Application-quota tracker) were added in v1.1 per ADR-0006 and are **Caregiver-only** as of v1.4 (ADR-0011); a **Provider consultation-slot scheduler** is added for the clinical tier. *(v1.4: the prior headline "thirteen" count is left loose pending the slot-scheduler's final factoring — the load-bearing decision is the module **boundary** (shared vs Caregiver-only vs Provider-only), recorded in ADR-0011 § Consequences.)*

**Deep modules:**

- **Booking lifecycle state machine.** Encodes the `requested → accepted | declined | expired → in-progress → awaiting-confirmation → completed | disputed | cancelled` graph. Inputs: current Booking + event. Outputs: next state + side-effects to enqueue. Independent of Stripe, persistence, and UI. *(v1.1: the Job / Application / Offer modules feed this machine at the `requested` entry point only. v1.4 (ADR-0011 / ADR-0013): **shared** across both supply roles — **Caregiver** hourly Bookings traverse the full graph, with `awaiting-confirmation` as the single ~24h review window (the sole dispute-bearing, payout-holding state); **Provider** consultations take the per-session path `accepted → completed` (auto-complete after the slot), carrying null payment and no in-progress / awaiting-confirmation / dispute.)* *(v1.5 (2026-06-10, ADR-0014): a recurring Caregiver arrangement is a **Booking Series** that materialises one ordinary Booking per occurrence — each traverses this same graph independently; the Series itself holds no lifecycle state.)* *(v1.7 (2026-06-23, ADR-0014 amended): a Parent may adjust an `accepted` Booking's duration — **extend** mutates it immediately; **shorten** writes a transient `pendingTimeChange` proposal the Caregiver approves/declines. This is a sub-state on `accepted`, not a new top-level status, and resolves back to a plain `accepted` Booking. A multi-day one-off bundle and a recurring Series both materialise many independent occurrence Bookings; the bundle carries no Series.)*
- **Caregiver Availability summary.** *(Rewritten 2026-05-19; scoped to Caregivers 2026-05-30, ADR-0011.)* A small pure module operating on the **Caregiver's** `availability_grid` (7-day × 3-band Morning/Afternoon/Evening boolean matrix) + `availability_note` (≤200 chars free text) + `paused` boolean. Functions: render-to-string (e.g., "Mon–Fri 3–5 PM"), intersect-with-search-query (a Parent's date/time filter intersects with the grid's day-band cells), is-paused (`paused=true` accounts do not appear in search). No slot CRUD, no block-on-request semantics, no automatic release. See `CONTEXT.md` § Availability.
- **Provider consultation-slot scheduler.** *(New 2026-05-30, ADR-0011 — slot-pick resurrected for the Provider role only.)* Operates on a Provider's published **consultation slots**: slot CRUD, mark open / held / released, intersect-with-search-query (match open slots to a Parent's date/time filter), and `paused`. Booking an open slot holds it and creates a per-session Provider Booking (null payment); cancellation releases it. This is the Provider analogue of the Caregiver grid — the two Availability shapes deliberately do not share a module.
- **Pricing & commission calculator (Caregiver-only).** Given the **Agreed Rate** (from the accepted Offer), hours, child count, per-child surcharge, Commission %, and category, produces Parent charge + Caregiver Payout amount + platform Commission + (optional) sales-tax breakdown delegated to Stripe Tax. Per ADR-0001. *(v1.1: input source flips from `published_rate` to `agreed_rate`; arithmetic unchanged. v1.4 (ADR-0011): Caregiver-only — Provider consultations carry no on-platform charge, Commission, or Payout, so they never enter this calculator.)* *(v1.7 (2026-06-23, ADR-0018): a post-session **Tip** is **not** an input to this calculator — it is a 100%-pass-through gratuity that bypasses the Commission skim entirely and is added to the Caregiver Payout as a separate, fee-free line.)*
- **Cancellation policy calculator (Caregiver-only).** Given a Caregiver Booking and a cancellation timestamp, produces refund and charge amounts under the v1 platform-wide rule (free ≥24h, 50% inside 24h, 100% inside 2h or after start). *(v1.1: refund math operates on the Booking's `agreed_rate`; no module change. v1.4 (ADR-0011): a **Provider** consultation cancellation has no fee math — it just releases the held slot and notifies — so it does not enter this calculator.)*
- **Disintermediation detector.** Regex-based scanner over message text. Outputs redacted text + match metadata (categories matched, original spans). Does not depend on storage; storage of original is the queue module's job. *(v1.1: now also invoked on every Offer's `scope_note` field at submit time.)*
- **Search ranking scorer.** `0.5 × distance_proximity + 0.3 × rating + 0.2 × recency_active_in_last_7_days` over a candidate Provider list, after filter intersection.
- **Rating reveal logic.** Given two-side Rating submission state and the 14-day window position, outputs visibility per supply profile and per Parent-facing-supply surface (asymmetric). Shared across both supply roles.
- **Verification workflow.** Per-**role** state machine — **Caregivers** (`role=caregiver`, Babysitter/Tutor/Nanny) and **Providers** (`role=provider`, clinical) both require email + phone + ID + **Checkr standard-package background screening** (county criminal 7-year + national criminal database + national sex offender registry + SSN trace; ~$30/check, charged at $35; see ADR-0007). **Providers** additionally require professional license + issuing state board (routed via the **per-state license-board adapter** for the Provider's resident state) + insurance. **Caregivers** may **optionally** upload certifications (CPR, First Aid, degrees) earning per-credential badges (not an activation gate); Babysitter/Nanny may carry a self-attested "Tax-credit-friendly" (W-10) badge and (rarely) a state home-childcare registration. State-pluggable: the screening step delegates to the vendor-agnostic background-check adapter (Checkr in v1); license verification delegates to the per-state license-board adapter; the deep module itself remains state-agnostic — it consumes verification *results*, not vendor APIs. Providers from states outside the launch adapter slate route to a "verification pending — state not yet supported" holding state. *(v1.4 (ADR-0011): keyed on `role`, not the removed `kind`; "Specialist" → "Provider".)* *(v1.6 (2026-06-17): adds a **`phoneVerified`** step (hard activation gate; phone optional on the form — client #1) and re-groups Caregiver certs under the **Credentials** umbrella — `type ∈ {title, certification, training}`, admin-gated, hidden until approved, clinical-title rejection — client #9.)*
- **Retention/erasure planner.** Given an account-deletion or consent-withdrawal event, outputs the set of records to soft-delete, pseudonymize, hard-delete, or retain — driven by a retention policy table (account 30d soft-delete; financial 7y pseudonymized; messages 3y; background-check raw 6mo; sensitive data on event). *(v1.1: Job descriptions follow the same retention rule as Messages — 3 years post last activity. v1.4 (ADR-0012): the "sensitive data on event" rule now targets the family profile's **Safety Behaviors** + `safety_behaviors_consent_at` (deleted on account deletion or consent withdrawal); there are no per-child records to erase.)*

**New in v1.1 (deep modules, per ADR-0006; all four are Caregiver-only as of v1.4, ADR-0011):**

- **Job lifecycle state machine (Caregiver-only).** Encodes the `draft → open → (awarded | expired | cancelled) → closed` graph. Inputs: current Job + event. Outputs: next state + side-effects (notification dispatch, Application auto-decline-on-award, Booking creation). Pure; independent of persistence and UI. *(v1.4 (ADR-0011): the prior slot-pick auto-Job entry is gone — slot-pick now creates Provider consultation Bookings directly, with no Job. Direct-Message Jobs are born in `awarded` at Book-request acceptance.)*
- **Application lifecycle state machine (Caregiver-only).** Encodes `submitted → (countered | awarded | declined | withdrawn | expired)`. Inputs: current Application + event. Outputs: next state + side-effects. Coordinates with the Job state machine when its Application is awarded (Job transitions in lockstep). Per-Job application count is exposed as a derived value for the 15-cap gate.
- **Offer state machine (Caregiver-only).** Encodes `pending → (accepted | countered | declined | expired)`. Inputs: current Offer + event. Outputs: next state + side-effects (notify counterparty; on accept, transition Application + create Booking with `agreed_rate = proposed_rate`; on counter, supersede previous Offer). Pure; per-child surcharge is provided as an input snapshot at creation time and held immutable for the Offer's lifetime. *(v1.7 (2026-06-23): adds a sender-initiated **`withdrawn`** terminal transition (from `pending` or `accepted`) — when withdrawing an already-`accepted` Offer, the side-effect **cascade-cancels** every Booking (or the Booking Series) materialised from it. A **multi-day one-off** Offer (`slots[]`) materialises **one Booking per slot** on accept, each independent, no Series — ADR-0014 amended.)* *(v1.4 (ADR-0011): `scope_type` is `hourly` only — the `per_session` variant retired with the off-platform Provider tier; the ad-hoc `child_count` + `child_ages` ride on Parent-sent Offers — ADR-0012.)* *(v1.6 (ADR-0017 / ADR-0016 / #4): the `countered` transition is **gated by the counterparty Caregiver's `negotiable` flag** — when off, only accept/decline are valid and the Direct-Message composer locks `proposed_rate` to the published per-category Rate. Offers also carry `safety_behaviors[]` (disclosed subset) + `service_address`.)* *(v1.7 (2026-06-23): adds a sender-initiated **`withdrawn`** transition (from `pending` or `accepted`) — withdrawing an already-`accepted` Offer cascade-cancels the Booking(s)/Series it materialised (via `offerId` back-link); a still-pending Offer can also be edited or deleted. - **Application-quota tracker (Caregiver-only).** Per-**Caregiver** monthly cap accounting. Inputs: Caregiver ID + current month + event (Application filed). Outputs: remaining-quota count + boolean "can-file". Storage is a simple counter per Caregiver per month with a monthly reset job. Pure logic; the storage layer is a thin adapter. v1 cap is 30; configurable per Caregiver (admin override) for re-tuning.

### Interfaces (high-level)

- The Booking lifecycle state machine is consumed by the API layer, which translates HTTP/RPC events into state-machine inputs and persists the resulting transitions. The state machine itself does no I/O.
- The Pricing calculator (Caregiver bookings only) is consumed by both the Booking creation path (to compute the authorization amount and display the charge) and the Session-completion path (to compute the final capture amount). The same module produces the Parent-visible charge, the Caregiver-visible Payout, the platform Commission split, and any applicable sales-tax lines (delegated to Stripe Tax). *(Provider consultations are off-platform — they never enter this calculator, ADR-0011.)*
- The Disintermediation detector is consumed at message-submit time, before persistence. The redacted message is what the recipient ever sees; the unredacted original goes to the flagged-thread queue.
- The Rating reveal logic is queried at every read — Provider profile views, Parent-facing-Provider surfaces — with the current submission state passed in. No background "reveal job" needs to flip visibility.
- The Retention/erasure planner is invoked on a scheduled job and on direct user actions (account delete, consent withdrawal); it emits the action plan that the persistence layer executes.

### Architectural decisions

- **Stripe Connect Express (US entity)** is the **Caregiver** billing rail (ADR-0001). **Caregiver** Booking payments flow through Connect with `application_fee_amount` skimmed from the **Agreed Rate** (from the accepted Offer; previously the Caregiver's Published Rate); the Payout releases same-day once the ~24h review window closes (ADR-0013). **Provider consultations do not touch Connect** — payment is off-platform, so there is no `application_fee`, no Payout, and no 1099-K on the clinical side (ADR-0011). Both the **Parent Subscription** and the **Provider Subscription** are sold on web (Stripe-hosted checkout / Stripe billing) to avoid iOS / Android in-app-purchase rules; the mobile app reads Subscription status but does not sell it. Form 1099-K issuance to **Caregivers** is handled automatically by Stripe.
- **Supply web portal is web-primary, with a mobile companion** in v1 (ADR-0005, supersedes ADR-0002). The single **React Native + Expo** binary serves Parents and both supply roles in role-aware shells; the role is chosen at sign-up and is permanent per account. Heavy onboarding stays web-only and forks by role — **Caregiver:** Stripe Connect KYC + Payout management; **Provider:** license/insurance uploads + Provider Subscription — with the mobile companion linking out to the web portal in an in-app browser. Run-the-day tasks are mobile-native (Caregiver: accept/decline Bookings, manage Availability, apply to Jobs, message, complete Sessions; Provider: publish consultation slots, manage consultation Bookings, message). **SMS-on-Booking-request** and **SMS-on-Job-awarded** (Caregiver) and **SMS-on-consultation-booked** (Provider) are mandatory in v1 because they're the most time-sensitive supply events. *(v1.4: Flutter → React Native + Expo per ADR-0010; "both Parents and Providers" → Parents + both supply roles per ADR-0011.)*
- **Job is the canonical anchor object for Caregiver bookings** (ADR-0006, narrowed to Caregivers by ADR-0011). Every **Caregiver** Booking traces back to Job → Application → Offer; pricing is negotiable via the Offer primitive (Accept / Counter / Decline pill buttons inline in the message thread). *(v1.6: negotiation is **per-Caregiver toggleable** — Counter is hidden and the Direct-Message rate locks when the Caregiver sets `negotiable=false`; ADR-0017.)* Per-Job cap is 15 Applications; per-**Caregiver** monthly cap is 30 Applications. Posting a Job requires an active Parent Subscription; applying to a Job requires the Caregiver's Verification to be `cleared`. **Provider consultations bypass the Job chain entirely** — they are slot-pick Bookings with no Job, Application, or Offer (the prior "slot-pick auto-creates a hidden Job" mechanic is gone — ADR-0011).
- **US-national launch jurisdiction from day one** (ADR-0009, supersedes ADR-0003). Checkr standard-package screening (multi-state) / **per-state license-adapter slate** populated at launch for priority Provider-supply states / federal compliance floor (COPPA + HIPAA-adjacent + FCRA + IRS + Title VII + CAN-SPAM + TCPA) + **state-privacy patchwork module** (CCPA/CPRA + VCDPA + CPA + CTDPA + UCPA + FDBR + OCPA + TDPSA + others routed per user state) / 3DS-optional payment posture / **Stripe Tax** for per-state nexus + taxability on Subscription and Commission / **per-state classification addenda** on Provider Terms for AB5/ABC-test states. Per-state compliance adapters are **core v1 deliverables, not Phase 2** — exercised at launch rather than abstract hooks.
- **Supabase Auth (US-region project)** is the identity provider per ADR-0010 (supersedes Firebase Auth from ADR-0004). Parent: Sign in with Apple + Google + email/password, device-trust SMS OTP. Supply (Caregiver & Provider): email/password + Google (+ Apple on mobile), web-side step-up MFA on payout-sensitive actions (**Caregiver only** — Providers have no payouts). Admin: TOTP MFA mandatory.
- **Daily.co** is the embedded video provider (US rooms).
- **All vendor configurations pin US data residency**; documented in the Privacy Policy vendor data-flow inventory appendix.

### Schema-shape implications (no specific column names)

- A Booking carries Category, Parent ID, **supply ID** (Caregiver or Provider) + `supply_role`, **`child_count` + `child_ages`** (ad-hoc; no Child IDs — ADR-0012), a planned **date + start–end time** (the Morning/Afternoon/Evening band is *derived* from the window for availability matching, not stored as the primary field — ADR-0014) with the supply member's home state captured for per-state compliance routing, a nullable **Booking Series FK** (set on recurring-arrangement occurrences), state, and a **role-dependent payment shape**: a **Caregiver** Booking carries a payment intent ID, **Agreed Rate** (from the accepted Offer), **Published Rate snapshot** (for audit), **Job ID**, and (for hourly) a Session sub-record (proposed hours + confirmed hours + confirmation timestamp); a **Provider** consultation Booking carries a **null payment intent, null Job ID, and a display-only session-Rate snapshot** (off-platform — ADR-0011). *(v1.6: a Caregiver Booking also carries `service_address`, revealed to the Caregiver at `accepted` — #4; the card-level approximate distance is computed from ZIP centroids — #3.)* *(v1.7 (2026-06-23): a Caregiver Booking also carries an optional **`tipAmount`** (post-session gratuity — 100% to the Caregiver, no Commission — ADR-0018), a transient **`pendingTimeChange`** while a Parent's shorten request awaits Caregiver approval (extend mutates `durationHours`/`endMin` directly — ADR-0014 amended), and an **`offerId`** back-link to the Book-request that materialised it (so withdrawing that Offer cascade-cancels the Booking).)*
- A **Booking Series** (new v1.5 — ADR-0014; Caregiver recurring arrangements only) carries: Parent ID, Caregiver ID, Category, the recurrence rule (start date + weekdays + start–end time + end date), the **Agreed Rate** (applied per occurrence), and the set of materialised occurrence Booking FKs. Each occurrence Booking carries the Series FK (nullable; one-off and multi-day-one-off Bookings have none). The Series holds no lifecycle state of its own — each occurrence runs the standard Booking state machine. *(v1.7 (2026-06-23): a Series materialised by accepting a recurring Book-request carries an **`offerId`** back-link so withdrawing that Offer cancels the still-upcoming occurrences.)*
- The **Parent profile** is a single family-level record: `bio` (free text), `preferences[]` (checklist), `safety_behaviors[]` (fixed checklist), and `safety_behaviors_consent_at` (timestamp). No `child_profiles` table exists (ADR-0012); children appear only as ad-hoc `child_count` + `child_ages` on Offers / Bookings. *(v1.6: adds an optional `default_address` (pre-fills the per-transaction `service_address` — #4); a parent-selected **subset** of `safety_behaviors[]` is copied onto a Job / Offer at compose for pre-application disclosure — ADR-0016.)*
- The **Tutor** single-child constraint (`child_count == 1`) is enforced at the Booking creation API boundary, not by trusting clients. (Provider consultations are inherently single-engagement — ADR-0011 / ADR-0012.)
- Disintermediation flagged threads carry the unredacted original, the redacted delivered text, the match metadata, and an FK to the message **or to the Offer** (since Offer `scope_note` is detector-eligible); T&S audit logs reference both admin ID and thread ID with timestamp + mode + reason. Thread ID keys off Job ID — every thread is anchored to a Job.
- Rating storage carries submission timestamp + reveal timestamp; reveal timestamp is computed by the rating-reveal module on read (no batch job).
- Retention is driven by a policy table with per-record-type rules; the planner produces actions, the persistence layer applies them. Job descriptions follow the Message-content retention rule (3 years post last activity) because the disclosure surface is the same; Application proposals and Offer `scope_note` fields follow the same rule.
- **Job** (Caregiver-only) carries: Parent ID, Category (Babysitter / Tutor / Nanny), scope description (free text), structured logistics (ZIP and a **concrete schedule** — a single date + start–end time for one-off (or **several dates each with a window** for a multi-day one-off, posted as one Job per date — ADR-0014 amended), or a recurrence rule [start date + weekdays + time window + end date] for recurring — ADR-0014), optional budget hint, state, consent-acknowledgement timestamp, posted-at timestamp, expires-at timestamp, awarded-Application FK (nullable), `entry_path` enum (`posted` | `direct_message`). *(v1.2: replaces the prior `is_auto` boolean tied to slot-pick auto-Jobs — that path is removed. v1.4: Caregiver-only — ADR-0011.)* *(v1.6: Job adds `safety_behaviors[]` (parent-disclosed subset) + `service_address`, and `child_count` / `child_ages` are populated at **compose**, not Award — ADR-0016 / #4.)* *(v1.7 (2026-06-23): the `radius` field is **dropped** from the Job (location is the ZIP; proximity is carried by the v1.6 service-address / ZIP-centroid distance — ADR-0014 amended), and the Job gains an optional **`dispute`** for a Parent's post-hoc billing dispute on a past Job — ADR-0013 amended.)*
- **Application** carries: Job FK, Provider ID, free-text proposal, current-Offer FK, state, filed-at timestamp.
- **Offer** (Caregiver-only) carries: Application FK (or `thread_id` for pre-acceptance Direct-Message), sender (parent | caregiver), `proposed_rate`, `scope_type` (`hourly` only), `scope_quantity`, the **proposed date + start–end time** (or a recurrence rule for a recurring arrangement; band derived for matching — ADR-0014), `child_count` + `child_ages` (ad-hoc, on Parent-sent Offers — ADR-0012), optional `scope_note`, `computed_total`, `per_child_surcharge_snapshot`, `valid_until`, state, supersedes-Offer FK (nullable, for counter-Offer chain), sent-at timestamp. *(v1.4: `sender` value `provider` → `caregiver`; `attached_child_ids` dropped in favor of count + ages; `scope_type` `per_session` retired — ADR-0011 / ADR-0012.)*
- **Application-quota counter** (Caregiver-only) keyed by (Caregiver ID, year-month); incremented on Application filing; reset by monthly scheduled job. Admin override path supported but not exposed in v1 UI.
- **Supply (Caregiver) record** *(v1.6)* carries `categories[]` (was a single `category` — ADR-0015), a **per-category Rate map** (was one `rate`), per-category `headline` + specialty, person-level `ages_served` + `behaviour_comfort[]` (drawn from the Safety-Behaviors taxonomy — #8), a `negotiable` boolean (default true — ADR-0017), and a `credentials[]` list (`{type: title | certification | training, status: pending | verified | rejected}` — #9). Providers keep a single `specialty` and are unaffected by `categories[]` / `negotiable`.

## Testing Decisions

### What makes a good test (in this codebase)

- **Test external behavior, not implementation details.** A test for the Booking lifecycle should assert that "an `accepted` Booking with a Parent-cancellation event 1 hour before start produces a 100%-charge cancellation," not that any specific function was called along the way. Tests that assert on internals make refactoring expensive and don't catch real regressions.
- **Tests are fast because deep modules are pure.** No database, no Stripe sandbox, no Supabase emulator in the deep-module test paths. Inputs in, outputs out, milliseconds per test.
- **Integration modules are not unit-tested in v1.** They wrap external SDKs and are mostly glue; testing them directly tends to retest the SDK. End-to-end smoke tests at the API boundary cover what matters there post-launch.
- **Property-based tests where they fit** — particularly Pricing and Cancellation calculators (numeric invariants like "Parent charge ≥ Caregiver Payout" or "cancellation refund + cancellation fee = original authorized amount"). The Booking state machine is also a natural fit for state-transition properties ("from any state, applying an `expire` event when the request is older than 24h reaches `expired` or no-op").

### Modules to test in v1

All deep modules (nine original + four Caregiver-only from v1.1 + the Provider consultation-slot scheduler):

1. **Booking lifecycle state machine** — exhaustive coverage of the state graph; round-trip and illegal-event tests.
2. **Caregiver Availability summary** — grid render-to-string, search-query intersection, paused-hides-from-search. **Provider consultation-slot scheduler** — slot CRUD, open/held/released transitions, booking-holds-slot, cancellation-releases-slot, query intersection. *(v1.4: the prior single "Availability calendar — slot CRUD/block-on-request" entry described the removed slot-pick model; it splits into the Caregiver grid module + the Provider slot scheduler — ADR-0011.)*
3. **Pricing & commission calculator** — base + per-child surcharge, Commission skim, optional sales-tax line delegation to Stripe Tax, rounding, edge cases (zero hours, fractional hours). *(v1.1: tests updated to use `agreed_rate` as the input field; arithmetic invariants unchanged.)*
4. **Cancellation policy calculator** — boundary tests at 2h, 24h, and after-start; refund/charge invariants.
5. **Disintermediation detector** — coverage of each pattern category; redaction-correctness; false-positive curation. *(v1.1: test fixtures extended to cover Offer `scope_note` inputs alongside Message inputs.)*
6. **Search ranking scorer** — score-stability, filter-match correctness, edge cases (no matches, ties).
7. **Rating reveal logic** — all four window/submission combinations across Provider-facing and Parent-facing surfaces.
8. **Verification workflow** — per-**role** coverage (Caregiver vs Provider); transition tests; optional Caregiver cert-badge flow + W-10 / FCCH badge flows; per-state license-adapter contract (one launch-state adapter + stub second-state adapter). *(v1.4: keyed on `role`, not `kind` — ADR-0011.)*
9. **Retention/erasure planner** — every retention rule; consent-withdrawal coverage; financial-record pseudonymization.
10. **Job lifecycle state machine** (Caregiver-only) — exhaustive state graph coverage (draft → open → awarded/expired/cancelled → closed); **Direct-Message Job entry path** *(v1.2 — replaces slot-pick auto-Job: a Direct-Message Job is created directly in `awarded` state at Book-request acceptance, atomically with its single Application + Booking)*; awarded-side-effect verification (auto-decline other Applications for Posted Jobs; Booking creation with correct `agreed_rate`); 14-day expiry path (Posted Jobs only); cap-reached behavior (Job at 15 Applications rejects new ones, Posted Jobs only).
11. **Application lifecycle state machine** — state graph coverage (submitted → countered/awarded/declined/withdrawn/expired); coordination with Job state machine; lockstep transitions on Award.
12. **Offer state machine** — pending → accepted/countered/declined/expired; counter-Offer chain (supersedes pointer); `valid_until` expiry path; `per_child_surcharge_snapshot` immutability across Offer lifetime; on-accept side-effects (Application transitions, Booking creation with `agreed_rate = proposed_rate`).
13. **Application-quota tracker** — per-Provider counter increments correctly; monthly reset boundary tests; cap-reached returns can-file=false; admin override re-enables filing; concurrent-filing race (counter is authoritative; quota is enforced server-side).

### Prior art for the tests

The codebase is greenfield (the **React Native + Expo** app scaffolds from a single entry today). There is no prior testing pattern to follow. Per ADR-0004, the **deep modules live in pure TypeScript packages on the backend**; client-side calculations in the React Native app go through the same API rather than re-implementing logic client-side, so the deep modules are tested once in TypeScript using **Vitest** (or Jest if the team prefers). React Native component / integration tests use the Expo + React Native Testing Library toolchain. State-machine and calculator tests lean on table-driven patterns; the Disintermediation detector uses a curated fixture set of message strings (extended in v1.1 to include Offer `scope_note` fixtures); the Booking, Job, Application, and Offer state machines and the Retention planner are all natural fits for property-based tests via `fast-check`. The Verification workflow's per-state adapter contract is tested via a launch-state adapter fixture plus a stub second-state adapter, to keep the pluggability honest.

**Added in v1.1 (Phase 2 candidates per ADR-0006):**

- **Direct invite to apply.** Parent reaches out to a saved or specific Caregiver asking them to apply to a posted Job. v1 Jobs are closed-list — applications come only from Caregivers who chose to apply via the open feed.
- **Per-Caregiver application credits / pay-per-lead.** Thumbtack-style supply-side economics where Caregivers pay per Application. v1 controls spam via the 30-Application monthly cap; the richer commercial mechanism is deferred for post-launch evaluation based on observed Caregiver behavior.
- **Partial-award Jobs.** A Parent awards multiple Providers from a single Job (e.g. weekend Nanny rotation). v1 supports the same outcome via multiple Jobs.
- **Single-account dual-role users.** A user who is both a supply member (Caregiver or Provider) and a Parent on Our Haven (e.g. a Babysitter who also wants to book a Tutor for her own child). v1 requires two accounts with different emails; dual-role per account is deferred because it materially complicates Stripe identity (Connect Express + Customer on one account), consent posture, and MFA orchestration.
- **Two-stage Job disclosure.** A "public brief" (sanitised) visible in the feed plus a "detailed brief" (full child context) unlocked by the Parent for shortlisted Providers. v1 uses free-text Parent-controlled disclosure with a one-time consent warning; the two-stage model is the privacy-correct Phase 2 architecture.
- **Role-switching post-sign-up.** A Parent who later wants to become a Provider (or vice versa) currently needs a second account; v1 has no in-app role conversion path.
- **In-thread Offer history view.** Beyond seeing the currently-active Offer bubble, v1 doesn't surface a structured "all past Offers in this thread" timeline; each Offer is visible inline in the thread but not aggregated separately.
